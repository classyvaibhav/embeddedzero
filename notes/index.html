Your journey: 12 lessons · ~10 weeks

💡What You'll Learn

By the end of this course you'll understand everything an entry-level Embedded Systems Trainee needs to know. You'll go from "what's a resistor?" to "I just built an ESP32 IoT sensor and wrote the code."

Your Starting Point You have a strong physics background from MSc Applied Physics. You know electromagnetism, semiconductor band theory, and maths. That's great — we'll connect new ideas to what you already know. The gap is just the hands-on practical side. This course closes that gap.
How to Use These Notes
  1. Read one lesson. Don't rush — take 45–60 minutes.
  2. Try the simulator circuit in every lesson. You can use Tinkercad or Wokwi for free in a browser.
  3. Do the "Check Yourself" questions at the end of each lesson. If you get one wrong, re-read that section.
  4. Write a tiny note in your GitHub README saying "Lesson X complete". Builds your portfolio.

A Note on Simulators In every lesson you'll see a "Try It in the Simulator" box with direct links. Two free options:
  • Tinkercad Circuits — best for Arduino + simple electronics. Colourful, easy.
  • Wokwi — best for ESP32. Faster, more sensors.
You do not need to buy hardware to start. Simulators work in any browser. (But buying the ESP32 kit is still recommended once you hit Lesson 7.)
