Schematic Symbols — Visual Reference

Every symbol she'll encounter in a real schematic. Open this as a second tab while reading datasheets or decoding circuits.

Section Index Overview Symbols Part 1 Part 2 Part 3 Part 4 Labs Cheats

01Passives

Resistors, capacitors, inductors. The most common components on any board.

Resistor (US)
Zigzag. Most common in Indian schematics. Label: R1 10k.
Resistor (IEC)
Rectangle. European standard, also common. Same meaning as US.
Potentiometer
Resistor with a wiper arrow. 3 pins: two ends + wiper. Volume knobs, adjustable dividers.
Capacitor (non-polar)
Two straight parallel lines. Ceramic, film, MLCC. 100nF, 1μF typical.
Capacitor (polar)
One straight + one curved. Electrolytic / tantalum. Polarity matters! +/− always marked.
Inductor
Series of loops. In switching regulators (buck), RF matching, filters. 10μH typical.

02Diodes

One-way current valves. Rectifiers, clamps, LEDs, Zeners, Schottky.

Diode (standard)
Triangle → bar. Current flows anode → cathode. Vf ~0.7V Si. 1N4148, 1N4007.
LED
Diode + two arrows (light emitted). Vf: red ~2V, green ~2.2V, blue/white ~3V.
Zener diode
Cathode bar bent into Z-shape. Operates in reverse breakdown as voltage reference. 5.1V Zener.
Schottky diode
S-shaped cathode. Low Vf ~0.3V. Power protection, rectification. 1N5819.

03Transistors (BJT & MOSFET)

Switches and amplifiers. Mnemonic: NPN arrow "Never Points iN".

NPN BJT
Arrow on emitter points out. Low-side switching. 2N2222, BC547.
PNP BJT
Arrow on emitter points in. High-side switching. 2N3906, BC557.
N-MOSFET (enhancement)
Arrow into channel. Low-side switch. Gate is high-impedance. IRLZ44N, 2N7000.
P-MOSFET (enhancement)
Arrow out of channel. High-side switch (source at V+). IRF9540.

04Amplifiers & ICs

Op-amps, comparators, generic IC representation.

Op-Amp
Triangle. − input top, + input bottom, output right. Supply rails often omitted. LM358, TL072.
Comparator
Triangle with step/hysteresis symbol inside. Digital output. LM393.
Generic IC
Rectangle with pins labelled. Regulators, MCUs, sensors. Always check datasheet for pinout.
Voltage Source
Circle with V. Ideal source. Used in simulation schematics.

05Power, Ground & Connectors

Rails, grounds, switches, batteries, headers.

Ground (signal)
Three horizontal lines stacked. Common reference for all voltages.
Chassis ground
Hatched hatchet under bar. Connected to metal enclosure. Not always the same as signal GND.
Power rail
Upward arrow or T with label. +3V3, +5V, VBAT, VCC.
Switch (SPST)
Single-pole, single-throw. Two terminals, open/closed.
Push button
Momentary switch. Bar pushed down when pressed. Used for RESET, BOOT, user inputs.
Battery
Long line = +, short line = −. Multiple cells = longer stack. 9V, 3.7V LiPo.
Fuse
Rectangle or oval with line through. Blows at overcurrent. F1 1A.
Header / Connector
Numbered pin rectangle. J1, CN1, P1. Programming / debug / I/O.
Crystal
Rectangle with symmetric leads. Clock reference. 16 MHz, 32.768 kHz.
Test Point
Circle labelled TP1, TP2... Hook for scope probe during bring-up and debug.
Transformer
Two coils with core bars between. Power supplies, isolation. Rare in modern embedded.

06Logic Gates

Digital building blocks. Mostly inside MCUs now, but still appear in glue-logic and discrete designs.

AND gate
D-shape. Output HIGH only when ALL inputs HIGH.
OR gate
Shield-shape. Output HIGH when ANY input HIGH.
NOT gate (Inverter)
Triangle + bubble. Inverts input. 7404 IC.
NAND gate
AND + bubble. Universal gate — can build any logic from NANDs. 7400.
NOR gate
OR + bubble. Output LOW when any input HIGH. 7402.
XOR gate
Exclusive OR. HIGH when inputs differ. Adders, parity. 7486.

How to Use This Page When Teaching

  • Before reading any schematic with her: open this page alongside. Point at each unfamiliar symbol; she names it aloud.
  • Quiz drill: Cover the names. Show just the SVG. She identifies within 2 seconds. Do 10 in a row, weekly.
  • Her mistake pattern: Most beginners confuse (a) NPN vs PNP arrow direction, (b) polar vs non-polar capacitor, (c) LED vs plain diode. Drill these three pairs hardest.
  • Missing from this page: specialised parts (thermistor, photodiode, relay coil, motor) — cover them if/when they appear in a real design.
